# Sponsored Brands

Sponsored Brands help brands build presence in shopping results through product collections, Store routing, brand-led creative, and video. Available creative controls vary by format, marketplace, and date; verify current Amazon Ads requirements before production.

## Role

Use Sponsored Brands when the goal is:

- Category visibility beyond one ASIN.
- Brand defense on branded searches.
- New-to-brand acquisition.
- Store traffic.
- Video-led product education.
- Cross-sell across a product line.

Sponsored Brands usually work best after the brand has retail-ready hero ASINs, a credible Store or landing page, and enough budget to separate brand defense from category growth.

## Formats

| Format | Best use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Product collection | Promote a set of products | Good for category terms and product-line comparison. Verify current marketplace rules; in the U.S., 2026 changes have been reported around AI-powered layouts and 3-10 ASIN product sets. |
| Store spotlight | Route shoppers to Store subpages | Use when Store navigation and category pages are strong; Store is required. |
| Sponsored Brands video | Educate quickly in search results | Strong for products where demonstration improves understanding; verify horizontal/vertical and video-only availability. |

## Landing Page Strategy

| Destination | Use when |
|---|---|
| Store | The shopper needs brand/category navigation or multiple products. |
| Store Spotlight subpage | The campaign promotes a specific Store category or collection. |
| Sponsored Brands landing page or custom Amazon URL | The campaign promotes a curated set of ASINs or a specific Amazon destination. |
| Product detail page | The campaign is built around one hero ASIN or video creative and the format supports it. |

Match the destination to the query. Category queries often need choice; specific product queries often need a direct detail page.

## Targeting

Sponsored Brands can use keyword and product/category targeting where available.

| Targeting | Use |
|---|---|
| Branded keywords | Protect brand demand and route shoppers to best destination. |
| Category keywords | Build category presence and new-to-brand demand. |
| Competitor keywords | Use carefully; requires compelling offer and brand comparison. |
| Product/category targeting | Useful for competitor, complementary, or category browsing context. |

## Creative Best Practices

### Headline

- Make the shopper benefit explicit.
- Align with query intent.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives, excessive urgency, emojis, and review claims.
- Do not write generic brand slogans if the query is product-specific.

### Logo and Custom Image

- Use a clean, policy-compliant logo.
- Use custom image where available to add context and improve differentiation.
- Ensure text is readable on mobile.

### Video

- Show the product and benefit in the first seconds.
- Design for sound-off comprehension.
- Use simple overlays and product-in-use proof.
- Route to the product or Store page that best continues the story.
- Distinguish video-with-products from video-only creatives where available.
- Verify 16:9 versus 9:16 support by marketplace and placement; vertical video can matter for mobile shopping.
- Common Sponsored Brands video planning specs include short duration, `.mp4`/`.mov`, strong captions, and high-resolution source, but final specs must be checked in the target account.

### Prompts and AI-Assisted Formats

- Sponsored Brands prompts became generally available in the U.S. in March 2026 for eligible Sponsored Brands campaigns, excluding some advertiser types. They can be automatically enabled and billed through CPC parameters where available.
- Product Collection controls are changing in some markets as Amazon adds AI-powered layouts. Do not assume custom headlines or lifestyle images are available for every Product Collection campaign; verify the current UI before writing creative specs.
- Amazon AI Creative Studio, image generation, and video generation can reduce asset production friction in supported markets, but generated assets still need brand, legal, and policy review.

## Campaign Structure

Recommended splits:

- Brand defense: branded keywords and own product terms.
- Category growth: non-brand category keywords.
- Competitor conquesting: competitor terms or ASINs.
- Video education: separate campaign for video to read creative performance.
- Store/category: separate campaigns by Store destination where budgets allow.

## Measurement

Track:

- CTR and CPC by query/target.
- Detail page views or Store visits.
- New-to-brand metrics where available.
- Brand impression share, page visits, and reserved share-of-voice options where available.
- ACoS/ROAS, but do not judge upper-funnel Sponsored Brands only by immediate sales.
- Query contribution to organic rank and branded search demand.

## Common Mistakes

- Sending all traffic to the Store homepage.
- Using one generic headline for brand, category, and competitor campaigns.
- Running video without a product demonstration.
- Mixing brand defense and category expansion budgets.
- Promoting weak ASINs with poor image, rating, price, or availability.
- Assuming Product Collections, Store Spotlight, and video formats share the same headline, image, and destination controls.
