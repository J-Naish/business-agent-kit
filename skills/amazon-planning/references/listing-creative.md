# Listing Creative: Images, Video, A+ Content, and Stores

Use this reference to plan Amazon product detail page creative. Creative requirements vary by category and marketplace; verify current Amazon creative guidelines before production.

## Main Image

The main image is a conversion and click-through asset. It must satisfy Amazon policy and make the product instantly identifiable in search results.

Common requirements:

- Pure white background for most categories.
- Product occupies most of the image area.
- No added text, badges, borders, watermarks, props, or inset graphics unless category rules allow them.
- Product must match what the customer receives.
- High enough resolution to support zoom where available.
- A practical planning target is at least 1,000 px on the longest side for zoom eligibility, with 2,000 px or higher commonly used for high-quality PDP assets. Verify current marketplace/category specs.
- JPEG is usually the safest default; TIFF, PNG, or GIF may be accepted depending on the upload path and category.

Planning guidance:

- Optimize for mobile search result visibility.
- Use the clearest hero angle, not the most artistic angle.
- Avoid packaging-only images unless the packaging is the product.
- For multipacks or bundles, make quantity unmistakable and compliant.

## Secondary Image Sequence

Use secondary images to answer shopper questions before they scroll to reviews.

| Slot | Role | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Main image | Clean product recognition |
| 2 | Benefit hero | Show the main reason to buy |
| 3 | Feature proof | Explain the differentiating feature or material |
| 4 | Use case | Show the product in context |
| 5 | Size/fit | Reduce uncertainty about dimensions or compatibility |
| 6 | Comparison | Clarify variants, bundle, or better-than-alternative logic |
| 7 | Instructions/trust | Show installation, care, warranty, safety, or contents |
| 8 | Reserve / surface-dependent asset | Reinforce aspiration or practical usage where the marketplace renders the slot |

Do not overload infographics. Text must be readable on mobile.

Do not assume every uploaded image slot will render on the live product detail page. Many marketplaces surface the first seven visual slots most prominently, and video can reduce visible image slots. Prioritize the first 6-7 assets.

## Product Video

Use video when motion, setup, before/after, texture, scale, or use case materially improves understanding.

Product detail page video upload often requires Brand Registry or equivalent brand/vendor eligibility. Verify access before building a video-heavy launch plan. Common planning specs include short 16:9 video, `.mp4` or `.mov`, high-resolution source, and marketplace-specific file-size/duration limits.

Recommended structure:

1. Show product and use case immediately.
2. Demonstrate the main benefit.
3. Prove differentiators with close-ups or real usage.
4. Address one or two common objections.
5. End with product, brand, and variant clarity.

Avoid unsupported claims, medical claims, exaggerated outcomes, and review-like statements unless allowed and substantiated.

## A+ Content

A+ Content should not repeat the bullet points in prettier form. Use it to:

- Tell the brand and product story.
- Explain premium materials, process, or technology.
- Compare products in the catalog.
- Reduce objections with diagrams, usage context, and care instructions.
- Cross-sell adjacent products.

Use Premium A+ Content only when the brand has enough assets and the product economics justify richer modules.

Premium A+ Content may be free during Amazon promotional periods once the brand meets operational eligibility requirements. Treat eligibility, not only budget, as the gating question.

## Stores

Amazon Stores are useful for:

- Sponsored Brands landing pages.
- Brand storytelling.
- Category navigation.
- New product launch hubs.
- Seasonal or event-specific pages.

Store planning should include page hierarchy, hero asset, category tiles, best sellers, comparison modules, and campaign-specific landing routes.

## Creative QA

Before launch, check:

- Mobile readability.
- Category compliance.
- Claim substantiation.
- Product/variant consistency.
- Localization quality.
- Image order logic.
- Accessibility of key product information without relying on tiny text.
- Consistency between ad promise and product detail page proof.

## Common Mistakes

- Designing for desktop when most shoppers see mobile.
- Using secondary images as generic lifestyle filler.
- Adding claims that legal or Amazon policy cannot support.
- Ignoring competitor image conventions in the category.
- Sending Sponsored Brands or DSP traffic to a Store that does not match the ad promise.
- Planning video, Premium A+ Content, or Store-heavy assets before confirming Brand Registry and account access.
