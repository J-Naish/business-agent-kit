# Sponsored Products

Sponsored Products are often the core Amazon Ads product for performance because they appear in high-intent shopping contexts. Amazon now describes Sponsored Products as promoting individual listings on Amazon and select premium apps and websites, with image or video formats where available. Eligibility, specs, and bidding options vary by marketplace and date; verify current Amazon Ads UI before launch.

## Role

Use Sponsored Products to:

- Capture high-intent search demand.
- Mine converting search terms and ASIN targets.
- Defend branded demand.
- Launch and rank new products.
- Conquest competitor product pages.
- Test retail readiness through click and conversion behavior.

## Placements

| Placement | Planning role |
|---|---|
| Top of Search | Highest visibility and often strongest intent; use for proven targets when margin supports higher CPC. |
| Rest of Search | Efficient scale and discovery. |
| Product Pages | ASIN conquesting, complementary products, and defensive coverage. |

Placement adjustments can amplify bids significantly. Current Amazon Ads surfaces may allow placement bid adjustments across Top of Search, Rest of Search, and Product Pages, but exact controls vary by marketplace and campaign type. Apply them only when the target has enough evidence.

## Targeting

### Automatic Targeting

Amazon matches ads to queries and products based on the listing and shopping signals.

| Match group | Role |
|---|---|
| Close match | Core query discovery. |
| Loose match | Broader query discovery. |
| Substitutes | Similar product pages and competitor alternatives. |
| Complements | Products used with or related to yours. |

Use automatic campaigns for learning, but harvest winners into manual campaigns for control.

### Manual Keyword Targeting

| Match type | Role |
|---|---|
| Exact | Proven queries and budget control. |
| Phrase | Controlled expansion around known phrases. |
| Broad | Discovery; requires negative keyword discipline. |

### Product Targeting

Use ASIN and category targeting for:

- Competitor conquesting.
- Own-ASIN defense.
- Complementary product placement.
- Category browsing reach.
- Price/rating/brand filtered opportunities where available.

## Bidding Strategies

| Strategy | Use |
|---|---|
| Dynamic bids - down only | Conservative efficiency and early testing. |
| Dynamic bids - up and down | Proven targets where Amazon's conversion signals are reliable. |
| Fixed bids | Control and experiments where you do not want algorithmic bid changes. |
| Rule-based bidding | Mature eligible campaigns where Amazon can adjust bids against a rule such as target ACoS. Verify eligibility, conversion thresholds, and supported targeting types. |

Choose bidding based on target maturity. Discovery campaigns usually need tighter risk control than exact winner campaigns.

## Recommended Structure

Minimum practical structure:

1. Automatic discovery.
2. Manual exact for harvested winners.
3. Manual phrase/broad for expansion.
4. Product targeting for competitor/complement/defense.
5. Brand defense if branded demand exists.

Larger accounts can split by product line, margin tier, match type, funnel role, and campaign objective.

## Search Term Mining

Weekly process:

1. Pull search term performance.
2. Identify converting queries with acceptable ACoS or strategic value.
3. Move winners to manual exact.
4. Add irrelevant or unprofitable queries as negatives.
5. Adjust bids by query economics and placement.
6. Feed high-value terms into title, bullets, A+ Content, Store, and Sponsored Brands where appropriate.

Do not graduate a query based on one order if the product has high variance. Use judgment based on spend, clicks, price, margin, and conversion pattern.

Useful reports include Search Term Report, Advertised Product report, Purchased Product report, Placement report, and Search Query Performance where Brand Analytics access is available. For automation or BI workflows, verify current Amazon Ads API and Selling Partner API availability.

## Newer Capabilities to Verify

- **Sponsored Products video**: video can appear on Sponsored Products inventory in supported accounts. Use when demonstration materially improves conversion.
- **Sponsored Products prompts**: in the U.S., Sponsored Products prompts became generally available in March 2026 and can be automatically enabled on eligible campaigns. They are billed through the campaign's CPC parameters and can be reviewed or paused in Ads Console/API where available.
- **Audience bid boosting**: AMC-built audiences can be used as bid modifiers for Sponsored Products in supported accounts. Treat these as bid boosts, not keyword or product targeting replacements.
- **Negative product and negative brand targeting**: use where available to prevent waste in product-targeting campaigns.

## Launch Budgeting

During launch, budget must support both discovery and harvest. If budget is very small, prioritize:

- One automatic campaign.
- One exact campaign for core terms.
- A small number of high-fit product targets.

Avoid spreading a small budget across every format and match type.

## Creative and Retail Readiness

Sponsored Products mostly use product detail page assets. Performance depends on:

- Main image.
- Title.
- Price.
- Reviews and rating.
- Delivery promise.
- Availability.
- A+ Content and secondary images after click.

If CTR is low, diagnose query/image/title/price. If CVR is low, diagnose product detail page, offer, review trust, and traffic intent.

## Common Mistakes

- Leaving automatic campaigns unmined.
- Letting broad campaigns consume budget after exact winners are known.
- Overusing high placement multipliers before targets are proven.
- Treating competitor terms as inherently profitable.
- Scaling spend while inventory or Featured Offer eligibility is unstable.
