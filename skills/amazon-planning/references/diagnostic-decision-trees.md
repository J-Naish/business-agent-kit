# Diagnostic Decision Trees

Use this reference before changing bids, budgets, listings, or campaign structure. Diagnose the likely root cause first.

## Start Here

| First question | If yes | If no |
|---|---|---|
| Is the product buyable and eligible? | Check traffic and conversion | Fix suppression, Featured Offer, inventory, account health, policy, or category approval first |
| Is inventory healthy? | Continue diagnosis | Reduce spend, protect exact winners, shift to in-stock variants, fix inbound plan |
| Is price/delivery/review trust competitive? | Continue diagnosis | Fix offer before scaling spend |
| Is tracking/reporting interpretable? | Continue diagnosis | Separate brand/non-brand, click/view, prospecting/remarketing, and attribution-era changes |

## Low Impressions

Likely causes:

- Bid too low.
- Budget too low.
- Targeting too narrow.
- Product not relevant to query/ASIN.
- Listing lacks indexed terms or attributes.
- Eligibility, suppression, or Featured Offer issue.

Actions:

- Check buyability and eligibility first.
- Broaden discovery through automatic, phrase, broad, or category targets.
- Improve title, attributes, and backend terms for relevance.
- Raise bids only on targets that are strategically relevant.
- Avoid adding many new campaigns if total budget is already too thin.

## High Impressions, Low CTR

Likely causes:

- Main image does not win the thumbnail.
- Title is unclear or missing the shopper's language.
- Price/rating/reviews/delivery are weak versus visible competitors.
- Query intent does not match product.
- Sponsored Brands creative is generic.

Actions:

- Test main image and title before only changing bids.
- Compare page-one competitors at mobile thumbnail size.
- Add coupon or offer improvement only if margin allows.
- Move weak broad terms to lower bids or negatives after enough data.
- Route Sponsored Brands to a more relevant Store page or landing page.

## High CTR, Low CVR

Likely causes:

- Ad promise and product detail page do not match.
- Listing does not answer objections.
- Price, delivery, review trust, or variation clarity is weak.
- Compatibility/size/content is unclear.
- Product is attracting the wrong query intent.

Actions:

- Improve first secondary images, bullets, A+ objection modules, and comparison chart.
- Clarify size, compatibility, ingredients, contents, and usage.
- Use review mining to find objections.
- Reduce or negate mismatched targets.
- Do not scale spend until CVR stabilizes.

## High ACoS

Ask which driver is responsible:

| Driver | Diagnosis | Action |
|---|---|---|
| CPC high | Auction too expensive for margin | Lower bids, shift to long-tail/product targeting, reduce placement multiplier |
| CVR low | Listing or offer problem | Improve PDP, price, reviews, delivery, relevance |
| ASP low | Basket value too small | Bundle, variant strategy, margin review |
| Attribution mix | Upper funnel or view-through blended | Separate click/view, prospecting/remarketing, DSP/STV |
| Launch objective | Intentional loss for rank/reviews | Keep budget cap and post-test decision rule |

## Rising TACoS With Flat Sales

Likely causes:

- Paid cannibalization of organic/branded demand.
- Brand defense overbuying.
- Broad campaigns not generating incremental demand.
- Organic rank not improving.
- Promotions masking weak base demand.

Actions:

- Separate brand from non-brand and exact from discovery.
- Review organic rank and SQP purchase share.
- Reduce spend on low-incrementality brand capture.
- Reallocate to category, competitor, creative, listing, or external demand only if conversion holds.

## Strong ROAS but Poor Business Outcome

Likely causes:

- ROAS ignores fees, returns, discounts, storage, and COGS.
- Retargeting bias.
- Brand demand cannibalization.
- Stockout or aged-inventory cost not included.

Actions:

- Use contribution margin and TACoS.
- Separate remarketing from prospecting.
- Add inventory and fee review to weekly operating cadence.
- Avoid scaling campaigns that look good only because they harvest existing demand.

## Stockout Risk

Actions:

- Reduce broad, category, and competitor spend first.
- Protect highest-margin exact terms and branded defense.
- Shift traffic to in-stock variants only if the shopper experience remains clean.
- Pause event pushes that will exhaust stock before replenishment.
- Re-forecast with actual ad-driven velocity.

## Sponsored Display Looks Too Good

Likely causes:

- Remarketing audience already had high purchase intent.
- View-through attribution is overstating incrementality.
- Prospecting and remarketing are blended.

Actions:

- Split views remarketing, purchases remarketing, contextual, and prospecting.
- Review frequency and audience overlap.
- Compare click-driven and view-through results.
- Use AMC or holdouts where feasible.

## Streaming TV / DSP Looks Weak

Likely causes:

- Wrong KPI: awareness judged by last-click ROAS.
- No lower-funnel capture path.
- Creative does not create memory or product understanding.
- Audience/supply/frequency too broad or saturated.

Actions:

- Review reach, frequency, branded search, DPVs, Store visits, and downstream sponsored ads lift.
- Add Sponsored Display/Sponsored Products capture.
- Improve first seconds of video and product/brand recognition.
- Use AMC or incrementality testing before declaring success or failure.
