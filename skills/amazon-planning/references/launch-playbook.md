# Launch Playbook

Use this reference for new product launches, relaunches, seasonal pushes, and marketplace expansions. Adapt timing to category, budget, inventory lead time, and marketplace rules.

## Launch Principles

- Retail readiness is the launch gate.
- Launch budget is for learning and controlled momentum, not guaranteed profit.
- Inventory and review trust must be planned before traffic scales.
- Ads should discover, harvest, and protect demand.
- Listing, ads, inventory, and promotion decisions should be reviewed together.

## Phase 0: Validation and Build

Before launch:

- Validate demand, competitor strength, price band, margin, review moat, and compliance.
- Define ASIN role: hero, trial, margin, replenishable, variant support, or clearance.
- Build title, bullets, attributes, backend search terms, main image, secondary images, A+ Content, and video if eligible.
- Confirm Brand Registry, category approvals, GTIN/GTIN exemption, and account health.
- Build inventory plan with weeks of cover, inbound timing, and stockout thresholds.
- Prepare Vine if eligible and economically sensible.
- Set break-even ACoS, launch ACoS tolerance, TACoS guardrail, and stop-loss rules.
- Prepare Sponsored Products automatic/manual, Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display, and Streaming TV/DSP only if the listing and budget can support them.

## Phase 1: First 2-4 Weeks

Operate tightly:

- Check buyability, suppression, disapproval, Featured Offer, inventory, and spend daily.
- Keep core Sponsored Products campaigns always on.
- Use automatic and broad/phrase campaigns to discover queries, but cap waste.
- Move converting queries and ASINs into exact or dedicated product targeting.
- Add negatives only after enough traffic to avoid over-pruning.
- Use coupons or intro discounts only when margin and inventory support them.
- Watch CTR and CVR separately; do not grade launch only by ACoS.
- Feed early search term and review language back into title, bullets, images, A+ Content, and Store.

## Phase 2: Stabilization

After initial learning:

- Reduce broad/automatic waste.
- Protect exact winners and high-converting ASIN targets.
- Improve main image/title if CTR is weak.
- Improve offer, bullets, images, A+ Content, price, delivery, or review plan if CVR is weak.
- Re-forecast inventory using real velocity.
- Split brand, category, competitor, discovery, and remarketing budgets when data justifies it.
- Decide whether the product deserves scale, hold, repositioning, or exit.

## Phase 3: Scale

Only scale when retail readiness, inventory, and query learning are credible:

- Add Sponsored Brands category and Store routing.
- Add Sponsored Display remarketing, own-ASIN defense, and competitor/contextual layers.
- Add AMC audience bid boosts or Sponsored Display audiences where available.
- Add Streaming TV / Sponsored TV when video creative and lower-funnel capture are ready.
- Test DSP Performance+ or Brand+ when signal quality, budget, and measurement support it.
- Use Manage Your Experiments for high-traffic listing elements.

## Seasonal and Event Launches

For Prime Day, Black Friday/Cyber Monday, holidays, or category-specific peaks, plan 8-12 weeks back:

| Timing | Work |
|---|---|
| 8-12 weeks before | Inventory, deal eligibility, compliance, creative refresh, Store/event page, keyword expansion |
| 4-6 weeks before | Discovery campaigns, audience building, Vine/review push where eligible, Sponsored Brands/SD setup |
| Event week | Budget pacing, stock monitoring, exact winners, deal-linked creative, suppression checks |
| 1-2 weeks after | Remarketing, repeat-purchase offers, search term harvesting, profitability and TACoS review |

## Launch Mistakes to Avoid

- Scaling paid traffic before main image/title/price/reviews/delivery can compete.
- Running out of inventory during the only period when rank momentum is building.
- Treating Vine as guaranteed positive review generation.
- Spending heavily on competitor terms when the competitor has a visibly stronger offer.
- Killing launch campaigns based on one or two days of ACoS.
- Running Sponsored Brands or Streaming TV without a strong landing path.
- Missing deal nomination windows for major events.
