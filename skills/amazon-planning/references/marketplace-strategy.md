# Marketplace Strategy

Use this reference before detailed listing or campaign work. It helps decide whether the product, account model, catalog structure, and marketplace posture can support profitable Amazon growth.

## Product-Market Fit Gate

Validate these before committing inventory or media:

| Gate | What to check | Planning consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Demand | Search volume, category traffic, SQP/Brand Analytics where available | Low demand requires external traffic or broader category positioning |
| Competitor strength | Page-one review count/rating, price, delivery promise, image quality, brand trust | A weak offer should not be pushed into expensive auctions |
| Differentiation | Visible benefit, patent/design/IP, bundle, material, use case, trust proof | If differentiation cannot be shown in image/title/A+ Content, ads will struggle |
| Margin | Contribution margin after fees, fulfillment, returns, discounts, and ad spend | Sets target ACoS, launch loss tolerance, and promotion limits |
| Compliance | Category approvals, safety, claims, labeling, import/tax, responsible person requirements | Compliance uncertainty should delay launch or limit scope |
| Operations | Replenishment lead time, FBA/FBM/SFP feasibility, support and returns | Inventory and account health are growth constraints |

Do not call a launch viable just because the ad product exists. Amazon is unforgiving when the product cannot win the visible comparison set.

## ASIN Role Design

Assign roles before allocating budget:

| Role | How to operate |
|---|---|
| Hero ASIN | Highest retail-readiness bar, strongest inventory protection, main ad budget |
| Trial ASIN | Small launch budget until conversion and review trust are proven |
| Margin ASIN | Conservative bids and promotions; protect contribution margin |
| Variant support | Use only for legitimate variations; do not use for review transfer |
| Replenishable ASIN | Prioritize Subscribe & Save, repeat purchase, BTP, replenishment audiences |
| Clearance ASIN | Controlled discounting and capped ads; protect brand perception |

## 1P, 3P, and Hybrid Posture

| Model | Best fit | Risks |
|---|---|---|
| 3P / Seller Central | Brands needing pricing control, launch control, margin visibility, direct ad operation | Operational burden, inventory/fulfillment responsibility |
| 1P / Vendor Central | Large brands where wholesale economics and Amazon retail relationship are favorable | Pricing control loss, co-op/chargeback pressure, CRaP-out risk |
| Hybrid | Mature brands splitting hero, new, premium, or channel-specific SKUs by economics | Channel conflict if the same ASIN is sold both 1P and 3P |

Default to 3P or hybrid unless 1P economics, operational benefits, and negotiation leverage are clearly superior. Do not put the same ASIN under competing 1P and 3P strategies unless the user has a deliberate channel-control reason.

## Brand Protection and Catalog Integrity

Brand Registry is the baseline, not the finish line. Where relevant, plan:

- Project Zero and Transparency for counterfeit-prone categories.
- Brand Catalog Lock or equivalent controls where available.
- Regular variation audits.
- Hijack monitoring.
- Clean trademark ownership and marketplace coverage.
- Exact brand name consistency across packaging, trademark, and listings.

## Variation Strategy

Use parent-child variations only when shoppers would reasonably view the children as variants of the same product: size, color, count, scent, style, material, or other category-approved themes.

Avoid:

- Grouping functionally different products to share reviews.
- Merging discontinued ASINs to transfer review equity.
- Using variation families to hide weak products.
- Creating parent structures that confuse reviews, Q&A, images, or compatibility.

Amazon's 2026 review-sharing changes make this especially important: reviews are increasingly kept product-specific when variations differ meaningfully in function or specifications. Audit variation families before launching ads or review programs.

## International Expansion

International Amazon expansion is usually a compliance and operations project before it is a media project.

Check:

- Tax/VAT/GST registration.
- Product safety, labeling, responsible-person, EPR, and import rules.
- Localized listing copy and customer support.
- Fulfillment model: local FBA, remote fulfillment, cross-border, or local 3PL.
- Currency, pricing, returns, and landed cost.
- Brand Registry and trademark coverage in the target marketplace.

Do not copy a working U.S. listing into another marketplace with machine translation and paid media. Localization, compliance, and delivery promise determine whether the ads can work.

## Exit and Enterprise Readiness

If the brand is building toward acquisition or enterprise planning, optimize for durable quality:

- Contribution margin, not GMV.
- Stable or declining TACoS at growing revenue.
- Lower SKU concentration.
- Clean account health and IP.
- Defensible reviews and repeat purchase.
- Off-Amazon demand and owned audience where relevant.

Avoid growth that only exists through discounts, inflated ad spend, or review-pooling tactics.
