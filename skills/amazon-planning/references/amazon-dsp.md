# Amazon DSP

Amazon DSP is Amazon's demand-side platform for programmatic display, online video, streaming TV, audio, and commerce audience activation across Amazon-owned inventory and third-party supply. It can be used by brands that sell on Amazon and by brands that do not sell on Amazon.

## Role

Use Amazon DSP when the plan needs:

- Upper-funnel reach beyond search-driven sponsored ads.
- Amazon shopping, streaming, and audience signals for awareness or consideration.
- Remarketing across longer consideration cycles.
- Streaming TV, online video, display, or audio placements.
- More advanced measurement such as Amazon Marketing Cloud, reach/frequency, path-to-purchase, or incrementality studies where available.
- AI-assisted DSP campaign types such as Performance+ and Brand+ where available.

Do not use Amazon DSP as a shortcut for fixing weak retail readiness. If price, availability, reviews, product detail page quality, or measurement are weak, DSP can scale waste quickly.

## Access and Budget

Access models and minimums vary by marketplace, account, and partner:

| Access path | Best fit |
|---|---|
| Managed service | Large advertisers that want Amazon or a managed partner to operate the buy |
| Partner or agency self-service | Brands that need DSP access and expertise without building an in-house team |
| Direct self-service | Teams with programmatic operations, measurement, creative, and budget discipline; access can be gated |

Always verify the current minimum spend, platform fees, service fees, and creative requirements for the target marketplace and access path.

Do not conflate "self-service" with "no partner required." In many markets, self-service DSP access is still commonly obtained through approved partners, while managed service minimums can be materially higher. Amazon is also moving toward unified Campaign Manager workflows, so UI boundaries between sponsored ads and DSP may shift.

## Format Planning

| Format | Primary use | Planning note |
|---|---|---|
| Display | Consideration, remarketing, retail media scale | Build multiple sizes and ensure product/offer messaging remains legible. |
| Dynamic eCommerce ads | Product-level remarketing and catalog-driven ads | Requires clean product feed and retail readiness. |
| Online video | Product education, category entry points, consideration | Use short, benefit-led creative with visible brand and product early. |
| Streaming TV | Broad reach and brand lift | Needs larger budgets and measurement plan beyond last-click sales. |
| Audio | Awareness and message reinforcement | Use simple claims and memorable brand cues. |

Streaming TV may also be available through a self-service Streaming TV / Sponsored TV campaign flow outside classic DSP setup. Check that option before routing small or mid-budget TV requests into DSP.

## Performance+ and Brand+

Performance+ and Brand+ are AI-driven Amazon DSP solutions that should be considered before defaulting to legacy DSP line-item planning.

| Solution | Role | Planning note |
|---|---|---|
| Performance+ | Lower-funnel and outcome-driven display/video/streaming TV optimization | Can optimize toward ROAS for Amazon sales or CPA for off-Amazon events where conversion signals are configured. Tactics can include acquisition, remarketing, retention, and consideration where available. |
| Brand+ | Upper-funnel awareness and reach/frequency | Useful when the goal is awareness, detail page views, or long-term engagement, not immediate last-click ROAS. |

Use Performance+ or Brand+ when the account has enough conversion, audience, and creative inputs to let the model learn. Keep human guardrails around exclusions, brand safety, frequency, inventory, and measurement.

## Audience Strategy

Common DSP audience groups:

- In-market shoppers.
- Lifestyle and interest audiences.
- Contextual audiences.
- Product views or category views.
- Brand purchasers or non-purchasers.
- Competitor or complementary product viewers where available.
- Advertiser first-party audiences where eligible.
- AMC-built rule-based and lookalike audiences where eligible.

Sequence audiences by intent. Do not mix prospecting and remarketing in the same line item if they need different budgets, bids, KPIs, or frequency controls.

## Measurement

Choose measurement based on funnel role:

| Role | Metrics |
|---|---|
| Awareness | Reach, frequency, video completion, branded search lift, brand lift |
| Consideration | Detail page views, Store visits, add-to-cart, new-to-brand, view-through quality |
| Conversion | Purchases, ROAS, ACoS, cost per acquisition, total sales impact |
| Incrementality | Geo test, holdout, Amazon Marketing Cloud analysis, matched-market test where feasible |

Be careful with view-through attribution. For DSP, direct attributed ROAS can overstate incrementality if the audience already had high purchase intent.

## Operating Cadence

- Launch with clearly separated prospecting, consideration, and remarketing line items.
- Set frequency caps that match purchase cycle and creative volume.
- Review creative fatigue, reach saturation, audience overlap, and supply quality weekly.
- Use Sponsored Products and Sponsored Brands search query data to inform DSP audience and creative themes.
- Use DSP insights to find new category, competitor, and lifestyle angles for sponsored ads and listing creative.
- Use AMC and unified reporting where available to separate direct attribution, assisted paths, reach/frequency, and incrementality questions.

## Common Pitfalls

- Starting DSP before sponsored ads and retail readiness are stable.
- Judging streaming TV only by last-click ROAS.
- Combining prospecting and remarketing into blended ROAS.
- Using generic brand creative that does not connect to Amazon shopping behavior.
- Ignoring inventory, price, and review changes during campaigns.
- Treating Performance+ or Brand+ as "set and forget" without checking signal quality, exclusions, frequency, and creative fatigue.
