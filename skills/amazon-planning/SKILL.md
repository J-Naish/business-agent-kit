# Amazon Planning

Use this skill to design Amazon marketplace and advertising strategy from business context through listing specifications, campaign structure, creative requirements, and operating guidance. It covers both marketplace readiness and Amazon Ads planning because ad performance depends heavily on product detail page quality, availability, price competitiveness, and review trust.

## Workflow

```
Step 1: Gather context       -> Business, product, marketplace, objective, budget, and KPI inputs
Step 2: Choose planning path -> Listing plan, ads plan, or integrated marketplace plan
Step 3: Design the system    -> Listing, creative, fulfillment, campaign, targeting, bid, and measurement design
Step 4: Produce outputs      -> Marketplace brief, ad plan, creative brief, and operating checklist
```

## Step 1: Gather Context

First look for any user-provided brief, product sheet, analytics export, category research, listing audit, or ad report in the current conversation or workspace. Do not assume a fixed local directory structure.

### Required Inputs

| Input | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Business context | Category, model, differentiation, channel mix, margin constraints |
| Product information | Product type, price range, benefits, variations, GTIN/UPC/EAN/JAN status, regulated category risk |
| Marketplace | Target Amazon marketplace, language, currency, local compliance constraints |
| Objective | New listing, listing improvement, ad launch, ad improvement, full marketplace plan |
| Budget | Monthly media budget, launch budget, creative budget, inventory constraints |
| Audience | Shopper segments, purchase motivations, objections, use cases |
| KPI | Target ACoS, ROAS, TACoS, sales, profit, new-to-brand, rank, review, or inventory goals |

### Useful Diagnostics

| Area | What to inspect |
|---|---|
| Account status | Selling plan, Brand Registry, account health, category approvals, advertising eligibility |
| Current performance | Sales, sessions, unit session percentage, ACoS, TACoS, CTR, CVR, reviews, Featured Offer eligibility |
| Listing assets | Images, video, A+ Content, Store, title, bullet points, product description, search terms |
| Fulfillment | Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), seller-fulfilled, hybrid strategy, inventory coverage |
| Measurement | Amazon Attribution, Brand Analytics, Search Query Performance, Amazon Marketing Cloud, MMP or external analytics |

### B. Amazon Ads Planning

Use this path when the account is retail-ready enough to run traffic or when the user needs a campaign architecture, targeting plan, budget plan, creative brief, or optimization rules.

| Ad product | Role | Typical prerequisites | Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sponsored Products** | Product-level ads in shopping results and product pages; usually the performance core | Professional selling plan, eligible product, Featured Offer eligibility for many placements | [sponsored-products.md](references/sponsored-products.md) |
| **Sponsored Brands** | Brand-level ads with logo, headline, products, Store, or video | Brand Registry and eligible Store/assets | [sponsored-brands.md](references/sponsored-brands.md) |
| **Sponsored Display** | Contextual, audience, remarketing, and display placements on and off Amazon | Eligibility varies by marketplace, seller/vendor status, and Brand Registry | [sponsored-display.md](references/sponsored-display.md) |
| **Amazon DSP** | Programmatic display, online video, streaming TV, audio, and audience activation | Self-service or managed access; larger budget and measurement discipline | [amazon-dsp.md](references/amazon-dsp.md) |

| Strategy area | Reference | Read when |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign architecture | [campaign-strategy.md](references/campaign-strategy.md) | Building funnel strategy, budget allocation, keyword strategy, seasonality, or launch phases |
| KPI and economics | [kpi-benchmarks.md](references/kpi-benchmarks.md) | Setting ACoS/ROAS/TACoS targets, break-even ACoS, benchmarks, or weekly operating rules |

### Business Goal to Ad Product

| Goal | Primary choice | Secondary choice | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capture high-intent demand | Sponsored Products exact/product targeting | Sponsored Display remarketing | Start with query and ASIN intent closest to purchase. |
| Discover converting queries | Sponsored Products automatic + manual broad/phrase | Sponsored Brands category terms | Use search term mining, then graduate winners into manual control. |
| New customer acquisition | Sponsored Products category keywords | Sponsored Brands product collection or video | Track new-to-brand where available and avoid judging only on short-term ACoS. |
| Brand awareness | Sponsored Brands video/Store spotlight | Amazon DSP online video or streaming TV | Use reach and branded search lift, not only direct sales. |
| Competitor conquesting | Sponsored Products product targeting | Sponsored Display product targeting | Compare price, rating, delivery promise, and reviews before bidding aggressively. |
| New product launch | Sponsored Products discovery + exact core terms | Sponsored Brands video, Vine, A+ Content | Do not scale spend before retail readiness, stock, and review plan are credible. |
| Repeat purchase and loyalty | Sponsored Display audiences | Brand Tailored Promotions, DSP remarketing | Match audience windows to purchase cycle. |

### Budget Posture

For small budgets, concentrate spend before expanding formats. Sponsored Products often provides the cleanest search intent and learning loop. Add Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display, or DSP only when there is enough budget, creative, and measurement capacity to give each role a fair test.

| Budget posture | Recommended structure |
|---|---|
| Very small / test | Sponsored Products only; separate automatic discovery and manual exact winners |
| Early growth | Sponsored Products plus Sponsored Brands or Sponsored Brands video for category presence |
| Scaling | Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display with clear funnel roles |
| Enterprise / full funnel | Sponsored ads plus Amazon DSP, Amazon Marketing Cloud, and incrementality testing where available |

### C. Integrated Marketplace Plan

Use this path for a new Amazon launch, major relaunch, or account rebuild. Design listing readiness first, then ads. Ads drive traffic; product detail page quality, availability, price, reviews, and delivery promise determine whether that traffic becomes profitable demand.

## Step 3: Design the System

### Listing Plan Sequence

1. **Account and eligibility**: selling plan, Brand Registry, category approvals, compliance, fulfillment choice.
2. **Product detail page**: title, bullet points, product description, search terms, variations, A+ Content.
3. **Creative system**: main image, secondary images, infographic logic, lifestyle context, video, Store.
4. **Offer economics**: price, promotion, fees, shipping promise, inventory, Featured Offer competitiveness.

### Ads Plan Sequence

1. **Campaign architecture**: split by role such as brand defense, category growth, competitor conquesting, discovery, remarketing, and launch support.
2. **Targeting design**: keyword match types, product targeting, category targeting, audiences, remarketing windows.
3. **Bid and budget design**: bid strategy, daily budget, placement adjustments, test budget, scale rules.
4. **Creative design**: format-specific copy, video, Store routing, custom image, and retail-readiness checks.
5. **Measurement design**: ACoS, ROAS, TACoS, new-to-brand, organic rank, inventory, contribution margin, incrementality where feasible.

### Naming Convention

Use consistent names that make reporting and bulk operations easier:

```text
[BrandOrProduct]_[AdProduct]_[Targeting]_[MatchOrAudience]_[Role]_[YYYYMM]

ProWidget_SD_Retarget_Views30d_202604
```

## Step 4: Produce Outputs

### Marketplace Specification

Include:

- Product detail page structure: title, bullet points, product description, search terms, variation plan.
- Keyword rationale: primary, secondary, long-tail, competitor, and negative themes.
- A+ Content or Premium A+ Content module plan.
- Creative brief: main image, secondary image sequence, video, Store modules.
- Fulfillment and pricing plan: FBA/seller-fulfilled/hybrid logic, fee risks, inventory coverage, promotion plan.
- Review and trust plan: Vine, compliant review requests, customer support, returns friction.

### Amazon Ads Operating Plan

Include:

- Objective, KPI, budget, and economic guardrails.
- Funnel role for each ad product.
- Campaign list with targeting, bid strategy, daily budget, match type, and negative strategy.
- Keyword and ASIN targeting list by intent tier.
- Creative specifications by ad product.
- Weekly operating checklist and optimization rules.
- Seasonality and promotion calendar.

## Platform Guidance

Amazon rules, fees, specs, and eligibility vary by marketplace, account type, category, and date. Treat all numeric specs as planning inputs that must be verified in the target account before launch. Avoid hard-coding one country, currency, Seller Central path, or local legal requirement unless the user explicitly targets that marketplace.
