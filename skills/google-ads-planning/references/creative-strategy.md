# Creative strategy

Use this reference when planning Search copy, P-MAX assets, Demand Gen, Display, Video, App, or Shopping creative. Creative strategy should define the persuasion logic before asset specs.

## Creative planning sequence

| Step | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Audience | Who is the buyer, and what do they already believe? | Persona / segment hypothesis |
| 2. Pain or desire | What problem, urgency, or aspiration creates demand? | Hook direction |
| 3. Offer | What is the concrete reason to act now? | Offer / CTA |
| 4. Proof | Why should the user believe it? | Reviews, numbers, demo, logos, credentials, guarantees |
| 5. Objection | What would stop the user? | Risk reversal, qualification, FAQ, comparison |
| 6. Format fit | What does the surface reward? | Search text, product image, Shorts hook, carousel story, etc. |
| 7. Measurement | What result would prove the angle works? | CTR, CVR, CPA, qualified lead rate, ROAS, lift |

---

## Angle library

| Angle | Works best when | Example use |
|---|---|---|
| Problem / pain | The buyer knows the pain but not the solution | Search, YouTube hook, Demand Gen |
| Outcome / benefit | The result is emotionally or financially clear | Search headlines, P-MAX text, Display |
| Proof / credibility | Trust is the conversion barrier | B2B, high-ticket, healthcare, finance |
| Offer / promotion | Price or urgency moves action | E-commerce, local service, seasonal campaigns |
| Comparison | The buyer is choosing between options | Search, landing page, carousel |
| Demonstration | Product value is easier to show than explain | Video, Demand Gen, App |
| Social proof | Adoption reduces perceived risk | SaaS, apps, consumer products |
| Risk reversal | Fear of loss blocks action | Trials, guarantees, returns, consults |
| Qualification | Low-quality clicks/leads are a problem | B2B, local services, high-ticket |
| Newness / novelty | The category rewards discovery | Demand Gen, YouTube, Shopping launches |

Do not create fifteen variants of the same idea. Create several distinct angles, then adapt each angle to the required asset formats.

---

## Business-model creative priorities

| Business model | Strong angles | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| B2B lead gen | ROI, operational pain, proof, integration, risk reduction, qualification | Vague productivity claims and low-intent lead magnets as the main pitch |
| Local service | Service area, speed, trust, reviews, license, emergency availability, clear pricing | Broad "best service" claims without local proof |
| E-commerce | Product use case, price/value, reviews, shipping/returns, comparison, bundles | Generic lifestyle imagery that hides the product |
| High-ticket | Proof, process clarity, consultation value, risk reversal, financing or qualification | Pushy urgency without trust |
| App / subscription | Core use case, habit loop, social proof, trial, app UI, retained value | Install-only copy when retention or subscription matters |
| Store visit | Nearby availability, inventory, directions, local offer, store trust | Brand-only messaging without local action |

## Format fit

| Format | Creative job | Design rules |
|---|---|---|
| Search RSA | Match explicit intent and qualify clicks | Use distinct headlines: keyword relevance, outcome, proof, offer, objection, CTA |
| Shopping | Make the product legible and competitive | Product title, image, price, shipping, reviews, and availability are the creative |
| P-MAX | Supply coherent asset groups | Align audience signal, search themes, LP, text, images, video, and product set |
| Demand Gen | Create or warm demand in feed-like environments | Use native visual hooks, product-in-use, short video, carousels, and social-style proof |
| Display | Remind, re-engage, or reach cheaply with guardrails | One visual idea, clear CTA, frequency control, placement hygiene |
| YouTube / Video | Earn attention before asking for action | Hook early, show brand/product early, build proof, close with a specific action |
| App | Show the post-install experience | App UI, gameplay/product moments, motivation, proof, fast mobile framing |

## Asset-group creative system for P-MAX

Each P-MAX asset group should answer one coherent business question:

| Element | What to align |
|---|---|
| Product / service theme | One category, service, margin tier, or customer type |
| Search themes | Terms that match the theme and LP |
| Audience signals | Customer list, remarketing, custom segment, or in-market hints relevant to the theme |
| Landing page | Commercial page that fulfills the ad promise |
| Text assets | Distinct angles, not paraphrases |
| Images | Product, use case, proof, and brand cues |
| Video | One clear hook and offer per concept |

Avoid a generic "all products / all audiences / all messages" asset group unless the account is too small to support segmentation.

## Demand Gen creative system

| Component | Practice |
|---|---|
| Seed audience | Start from high-quality customers, purchasers, qualified leads, or retained users |
| Hook | Use visual or text hooks that would work in a social feed |
| Proof | Use reviews, product demos, UGC-style scenes, customer logos, or before/after process clarity where policy allows |
| Offer path | Make the next step obvious: shop, compare, book, download, trial, quote |
| Surface coverage | Build vertical, square, and landscape variants intentionally, not by cropping blindly |
| Refresh | Rotate concepts when fatigue appears; replace ideas, not just colors |

For cold prospecting, do not lead with a generic brand claim. Lead with a pain, use case, product moment, proof point, or offer.

## Search copy design

Build RSA assets from different jobs:

| Asset job | Example direction |
|---|---|
| Relevance | Product/service phrase in buyer language |
| Outcome | What improves after purchase or inquiry |
| Proof | Rating, customer count, credential, case result |
| Offer | Trial, quote, discount, bundle, consultation |
| Qualification | Area served, price tier, business size, eligibility |
| Objection handling | No contract, fast setup, warranty, transparent pricing |
| CTA | Get quote, book demo, shop, compare, install |

Pin only when legal, brand, or clarity requirements demand it. Otherwise, preserve combination variety.

## Video concept checklist

| Section | Question |
|---|---|
| First 1-3 seconds | Is there motion, product, problem, face, or visual contrast? |
| First 5 seconds | Does the user know what this is and why it matters? |
| Brand memory | Is the brand/product visible early enough to be remembered? |
| Proof | Is there a reason to believe the claim? |
| Action | Is the next step specific and visible? |
| Mobile fit | Does text remain legible and important content stay in safe zones? |
| Variants | Are concepts meaningfully different, not just resized? |

## Creative testing

| Test type | Good question | Poor question |
|---|---|---|
| Hook test | Does problem-led beat outcome-led? | Does blue beat green? |
| Offer test | Does free consult beat pricing guide? | Does "Learn more" beat "Learn more today"? |
| Proof test | Do reviews beat customer logos? | Does one minor wording change matter? |
| Format test | Does vertical UGC-style video beat polished landscape? | Does the same asset cropped three ways perform differently? |
| Qualification test | Does adding price/service-area improve lead quality? | Does broader copy get more clicks? |

Judge creative by the metric that matches its job. Prospecting creative may first move CTR, engaged visits, assisted conversions, or brand search before direct CPA stabilizes.

---

## Creative brief minimum

When a creative brief is needed, include:

1. Campaign role and funnel stage.
2. Audience and trigger.
3. Primary offer and CTA.
4. Three to five distinct creative angles.
5. Proof assets available.
6. Objections to address.
7. Required formats and safe-zone constraints.
8. Measurement plan and refresh cadence.
