# Policy and Account Requirements

Use this reference before recommending X Ads for regulated categories, premium products, or accounts with unclear eligibility.

Policy source: X Advertising Policies (https://business.x.com/en/help/ads-policies), Cause-Based Advertising Policy (https://business.x.com/en/help/ads-policies/ads-content-policies/cause-based-advertising.html), Housing/Lending/Credit Opportunities Policy (https://business.x.com/en/help/ads-policies/campaign-considerations/housing-lending-and-employment-opportunities), Healthcare Policy (https://business.x.com/en/help/ads-policies/ads-content-policies/healthcare), and campaign targeting docs (https://business.x.com/en/help/campaign-setup/campaign-targeting). Last verified: 2026-04.

## Account Eligibility

X accounts and ads are subject to review before and during promotion. Before launch, confirm:

- Ads account is eligible, funded, and not under review/offboarding.
- Brand handle is appropriate for the campaign and has enough profile credibility.
- Verified Organizations or X Premium status if the account, product, or managed workflow requires it.
- Domain, landing page, app store listing, product catalog, and disclosures are consistent with the promoted claim.
- Managed products have X account-team, X Next, or support access confirmed before they appear in the plan.

Do not present Verified Organizations or X Premium as universally required for all X Ads. Treat them as credibility/eligibility inputs that can matter more for managed, branded, or sensitive workflows.

## Restricted and Sensitive Categories

X restricted categories can require country-specific rules, mandatory warnings, pre-authorization, or campaign limitations.

| Category | Planning rule |
|---|---|
| Housing, Lending, and Credit opportunities | US/Canada advertisers must obtain certification and cannot use prohibited targeting such as age, language, sex, protected traits, or zip-code-level precise location |
| Cause-Based Advertising | Requires advertiser certification and targeting limits; outside the US, geo is limited to state/province/region level or above, and only geo, keyword, and interest targeting are allowed |
| Healthcare and pharmaceuticals | Country-by-country eligibility; many products require prior authorization or are prohibited unless explicitly allowed |
| Financial products and services | Verify country rules, disclosures, targeting limits, and pre-authorization before planning |
| Alcohol, gambling, adult, tobacco, drugs, political content | Treat as restricted or prohibited until current X policy and local law confirm eligibility |
| Sensitive categories targeting | Do not target or infer sensitive attributes beyond X policy allowances |

If a campaign touches one of these categories, check the exact current X policy page and local law before selecting objective, targeting, creative, or claims.

## Policy Planning Checklist

| Check | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Category classification | Determines whether the campaign is prohibited, restricted, or pre-authorized |
| Market and language | X policy can vary by country and local law |
| Targeting constraints | Some categories remove or limit audience features |
| Claims and substantiation | Health, finance, employment, and cause-based claims need evidence and disclosures |
| Landing-page compliance | X reviews the destination, not just the ad |
| Approval timeline | Pre-authorization and managed approvals can break launch schedules |

## How to Write Plans Safely

- State policy assumptions explicitly.
- Use "verify current X policy/account eligibility before launch" for restricted categories.
- Avoid recommending microtargeting for sensitive or regulated categories.
- Do not suggest workarounds for policy restrictions.
- For urgent launches, include policy review as a gating task in the timeline.
