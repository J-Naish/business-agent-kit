# Branded Hashtags and Branded Features

Branded Hashtags let advertisers add a visually distinctive branded element to a hashtag across X. Current official X ad-format pages still list Branded Hashtags, while public reporting indicates ordinary hashtags in paid ad copy have been restricted. Treat managed Branded Hashtags as a separate X-approved product, not as permission to add hashtags to standard promoted posts.

## Availability

Branded Hashtags are typically managed-service products and may require X approval. Confirm current eligibility, markets, dates, creative requirements, and pricing with X.

Related managed branded features can include Hashmoji, Hashfetti, Custom Likes / Branded Likes, or other X-approved visual elements. Verify the current product names and markets with X before presenting them as available.

## Best Uses

- Brand launches.
- Entertainment and sports moments.
- Fan participation campaigns.
- Product drops.
- Social challenges or community campaigns.
- Event conversation hubs.

## Hashtag Design

| Principle | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Short | Easy to type and remember |
| Clear | The meaning should be obvious |
| Brand-linked | Connected to the brand, campaign, or moment |
| Safe | Avoid ambiguity, negative readings, or hijack risk |
| Global-ready | Check language and cultural meaning if running across markets |

Current indexed X specs for Branded Hashtags include:

- Up to 5 hashtags associated with an emoji.
- Desired hashtag should not exceed 250 mentions on X over the previous 30 days unless directly related to brand conversation.
- Emoji design at 72x72 pixels and visible at 16x16.
- Must work on light and dark backgrounds.
- Simple design; avoid multiple faces/logos that become indistinguishable when small.

## Creative Practices

- Use the hashtag as the campaign's conversation anchor.
- Pair with Promoted Ads to explain the reason to participate.
- Keep ordinary promoted ad copy free of hashtags unless the managed branded feature workflow requires it.
- Use creator, publisher, event, or community support when relevant.
- Prepare community management and response guidelines.

## Measurement

| Metric | Use |
|---|---|
| Hashtag usage | Participation volume |
| Reach and impressions | Conversation scale |
| Engagement | Quality of participation |
| Sentiment / content review | Brand safety and resonance |
| Downstream traffic or conversion | Business effect |

## Common Mistakes

- Hashtag is too long.
- Hashtag is clever but unclear.
- No participation prompt.
- No moderation plan.
- Treating hashtag volume as business impact without downstream measurement.
