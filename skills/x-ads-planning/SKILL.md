# X Ads Planning

Plan X Ads programs from strategy through launch handoff. Choose the right mix of X ad formats, define campaign structure, and create practical creative, copy, targeting, bidding, and measurement guidance.

## Workflow

```
Step 1: Information gathering       -> Business context, goal, budget, audience, assets
    ↓
Step 2: Format and objective choice -> Match objectives and formats to the job
    ↓
Step 3: Strategy and creative design -> Structure, targeting, bidding, measurement, creative
    ↓
Step 4: Delivery                    -> Recommendation, plan, operating spec, or creative brief
```

---

## Step 1: Information Gathering

Use available project context first. Ask only for missing information that affects the recommendation.

### Required Inputs

| Category | What to confirm |
|---|---|
| Business context | Industry, product/service, buyer, positioning, USP |
| Goal | Awareness, website traffic, engagement, app installs, app re-engagements, website conversions, lead generation, e-commerce revenue |
| Budget | Monthly or daily media budget |
| Audience | Geography, language, age/gender if relevant, interests, keywords, follower look-alikes, customer segments |
| KPI | Target CPM, CPV, CPC, CPE, CPA, ROAS, CPI, CTR, CVR, or qualified lead rate |
| Assets | Existing posts, images, videos, product catalog, app links, landing pages |

### Useful Context

| Category | What to confirm |
|---|---|
| Existing X Ads activity | Historical CTR, CVR, CPA, ROAS, CPE, CPV, creative learnings |
| Cross-channel results | Google, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, email, organic social, or CRM performance |
| X account status | X Premium, Verified Organizations, follower base, organic engagement |
| Measurement | X Pixel, Conversion API, app MMP, product catalog, UTMs |
| Competitive context | Competitors' visible activity on X and likely conversation spaces |
| Policy/compliance | Category restrictions, regulated claims, disclosure requirements, brand safety constraints |

## Step 2: Format and Objective Choice

X Ads supports both **self-serve** buying through X Ads Manager and **managed-service / reservation-style** placements for premium inventory.

| Buying path | Use when |
|---|---|
| Self-serve via X Ads Manager | Performance campaigns, testing, flexible budgets, always-on programs |
| Managed service | Large reach plays, premium placements, sponsorships, takeovers, negotiated programs |

### Official Campaign Objective Families

| Funnel | X objective | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Reach | Maximize reach and efficient exposure |
| Consideration | Video views | Get people to watch video |
| Consideration | Website traffic | Drive visits to a website or landing page |
| Consideration | Pre-roll views | Align video ads with premium publisher content |
| Consideration | Engagement | Drive post engagement |
| Consideration | App installs | Drive app installs |
| Conversion | App re-engagements | Drive action from existing app users |
| Conversion | Website conversions | Generate tracked website conversions |

Verify current objective availability in X Ads Manager because campaign types and eligibility can vary by account, region, and buying path.

### X Ad Features

| Feature | Use |
|---|---|
| Website Cards | Add click-through functionality to image or video ads |
| App Cards | Link to an App Store or Google Play destination, or deep link into an installed app |
| Conversation Buttons | Encourage users to post with prefilled text or a hashtag |
| Polls | Add two to four choices for interactive engagement |

### Recommended Mix by Business Goal

| Business goal | Recommended formats | Recommended objective |
|---|---|---|
| Maximize awareness | Vertical Video Ads + Video Ads | Reach or Video views |
| Product education | Image Ads + Carousel Ads + Video Ads | Reach, Video views, or Website traffic |
| Website traffic | Image/Video Ads with Website Cards | Website traffic |
| App installs | Video Ads, Vertical Video Ads, Carousel Ads with App Cards | App installs |
| E-commerce revenue | Dynamic Product Ads + Collection Ads | Website conversions |
| Lead generation | Image/Video Ads with Website Cards | Website traffic or Website conversions |
| Engagement | Text/Image/Video Ads with Polls or Conversation Buttons | Engagement |
| Event promotion | X Live + Video Ads + optional Takeover | Reach or Video views |
| Search-intent capture | Search Keyword Ads | Website traffic or Website conversions |

After selecting formats, present:

1. Recommended objective and format mix.
2. Why each format fits the business goal.
3. Approximate budget role for each format.
4. Measurement plan and primary KPI.

## Step 3: Strategy and Creative Design

Use these references as needed:

| Theme | Reference |
|---|---|
| Campaign structure, targeting, bidding, budget, measurement | [campaign-strategy.md](references/campaign-strategy.md) |
| Creative principles, Aesthetic Score, copy, CTA, testing | [creative-fundamentals.md](references/creative-fundamentals.md) |
| Format-specific design | The format reference files linked in Step 2 |

### Campaign Structure

- X Ads Manager uses a three-level structure: **Campaign -> Ad group -> Ad**.
- The campaign objective is the strategic anchor.
- Use Campaign Budget Optimization when the campaign should distribute budget across ad groups automatically.
- Use ad-group-level budget control when strict allocation, testing, or audience separation matters.

### Targeting Design

Common X targeting inputs include:

- Location, language, device, age, and gender where available.
- Keywords, conversations, events, interests, movies/TV, and follower look-alikes.
- Custom Audiences from customer lists, website visitors, app activity, and engagement.
- Retargeting and exclusions where the account and policy context support them.

Avoid over-fragmenting targeting unless there is a real budget, message, compliance, or measurement reason.

### Creative Design Principles

Use X's **Aesthetic Score** guidance as a quality filter:

- Avoid hashtags in ad copy unless the format explicitly needs one.
- Keep emojis minimal and brand-appropriate.
- Do not paste raw URLs into post copy when a Website Card or App Card is available.
- Use clean, polished creative with correct grammar and punctuation.
- Keep each ad focused on one message.
- Refresh creative regularly when frequency, CTR, CPE, CPA, or qualitative feedback suggests fatigue.

### Naming and Tracking

Use consistent, English-based names for filtering and exports.

```text
Campaign: X_{Objective}_{Audience}_{Format}_{Note}
Ad group: AG_{Audience}_{Targeting}_{Note}
Ad: Ad_{Format}_{Concept}_{Variant}
```

Recommended UTM pattern:

```text
utm_source=x
utm_medium=paid_social
utm_campaign={campaign_name}
utm_content={ad_name}
utm_term={keyword}
```

Use `utm_term` only when keyword-level reporting matters.

## Step 4: Delivery

Produce the output that fits the request: a short recommendation, structured inline plan, strategy memo, operating specification, or creative brief. Do not force a written document unless the user asked for one or the deliverable needs to be handed off.

A full plan usually includes:

1. Strategy summary: business goal, audience, and chosen objective.
2. Format mix: selected X ad formats and the job of each.
3. Campaign structure: campaigns, ad groups, budgets, targeting, bidding, and schedule.
4. Creative specification: aspect ratios, sizes, copy rules, CTA, and concept list.
5. Measurement design: X Pixel / Conversion API / app tracking / catalog events, attribution assumptions, and UTMs.
6. Operating cadence: launch checks, learning period, optimization rhythm, and reporting view.

Put any files where the user requested, or follow the host project's normal artifact conventions if they are already clear.

## Platform Notes

- X Ads product names, eligibility, specs, and managed-service availability change over time. Verify current availability in X Ads Manager or with an X representative before final launch.
- Premium products such as Timeline Takeover, Spotlight Takeover, Amplify Sponsorship, Branded Hashtags, and some Branded Notifications may require managed-service access or account eligibility.
- Benchmarks vary heavily by market, category, creative, objective, bidding, and measurement quality. Treat CPM, CPC, CPV, CPA, and ROAS benchmarks as planning inputs, not promises.
