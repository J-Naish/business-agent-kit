# Creative Fundamentals

Use this reference before writing TikTok Ads copy or specifying creative assets.

## Core Principle

TikTok's durable creative principle is: **Don't Make Ads. Make TikToks.**

Creative should feel native to TikTok: fast, human, useful, entertaining, specific, and designed for sound-on mobile viewing while still understandable with captions.

## Creative System

| Layer | What to design |
|---|---|
| Concept | The persuasion idea: pain, outcome, proof, comparison, demo, creator story, offer |
| Hook | First 1-3 seconds or first visual frame |
| Body | Demo, story, proof, objection handling, product use, social proof |
| CTA | The next action: shop, sign up, download, learn, message, watch LIVE |
| Variant | Creator, opening line, visual angle, product shot, edit pace, CTA, caption |

Test concepts before cosmetic variants. A new color, thumbnail, or caption is not a meaningful new concept if the persuasion logic is identical.

## Hook Types

| Hook | Use when |
|---|---|
| Problem callout | The audience has a clear pain |
| Outcome first | The desired result is visually or emotionally strong |
| Before/after | Transformation is credible and compliant |
| Myth busting | The category has misconceptions |
| POV / confession | Creator-led trust matters |
| Demo first | Product utility can be shown quickly |
| Social proof | Reviews, comments, creator endorsement, or usage volume matter |
| Search-intent answer | The creative supports Search Ads |

## Recommended Video Structure

```text
0-3s: Hook and context
3-12s: Product, problem, demo, or proof
12-20s: Objection handling, differentiator, or social proof
Final seconds: CTA and destination match
```

Shorter can work when the offer is simple. Longer can work when education, trust, or demo depth is needed.

## Sound and Captions

- Use audio intentionally; TikTok is a sound-aware platform.
- Use TikTok's Commercial Music Library or properly licensed audio for ads.
- Check current Commercial Music Library terms before production. TikTok refreshed music terms in 2025, and some Premium tracks can carry additional fees or buying restrictions.
- Add captions or text overlays so the message is understandable without sound.
- Avoid tiny text and UI-obstructed text areas.
- Match the audio mood to the product, audience, and funnel stage.

## Creator and UGC Guidance

Creator-style ads often work because they lower resistance and show product context. Good creator briefs include:

- Audience and use case.
- Problem/insight to dramatize.
- Required claims and prohibited claims.
- Product proof or demo requirement.
- Hook options.
- CTA and destination.
- Disclosure and usage rights requirements.

Do not over-script creators to the point that the content stops feeling native.

## Creative Fatigue

TikTok creative can fatigue quickly, especially with higher budgets. Watch:

- CTR decline.
- Hook retention decline.
- Rising CPM/CPC/CPA.
- Comment quality decline.
- Frequency and repeated audience exposure.

Refresh by changing the hook, creator, proof, offer, edit structure, or product angle. Minor copy edits rarely solve real fatigue.

## Copy and CTA

| Element | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Caption | Short, specific, and matched to the video |
| CTA button | Use the platform preset that matches the destination |
| On-screen text | Use for hook, proof, step labels, offer, or key objection |
| Hashtags | Use only when they improve discovery/context; do not rely on them as targeting |
| Claims | Keep substantiated and policy-compliant |

## Creative QA

Before launch, check:

- The product/offer is understandable in the first seconds.
- The creative matches the destination and optimization event.
- Captions/text do not conflict with UI safe zones.
- Audio rights are valid for advertising.
- AI-generated or Symphony-assisted assets have been reviewed for rights, disclosure, brand safety, and policy compliance.
- Creator permissions and Spark Ads authorization are in place where needed.
- Regulated claims and disclosures are handled for the target markets.
