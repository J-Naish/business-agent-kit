# In-Feed Ads

In-Feed Ads are standard TikTok video ads that appear in the For You feed and other eligible feed environments. They are the default flexible format for performance, testing, and always-on programs.

## Best Uses

- Website traffic and conversions.
- App installs and app events.
- Product demos and creator-style ads.
- Lead generation support.
- Retargeting and sequential messaging.
- Creative testing before scaling into broader campaign types.

## Specs and Setup

Use current TikTok Ads Manager specs before production. Common planning assumptions:

| Item | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Creative | Vertical video is the default creative posture |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 preferred; 1:1 and 16:9 may be supported depending on placement |
| Length | Non-Spark auction video can run up to 10 minutes in current guidance; 9-15 seconds remains a common performance sweet spot |
| Audio | Use licensed audio; avoid unlicensed commercial music |
| CTA | Select from TikTok Ads Manager presets |
| Destination | Website, app, TikTok Shop, lead form, or other eligible destination |

## Creative Practices

- Make the first 1-3 seconds carry the hook.
- Show the product, creator, or outcome early.
- Use captions or text overlays.
- Avoid long brand intros.
- Make the ad feel like a TikTok, not a repurposed TV or display ad.
- Test several distinct concepts, not only minor edits.

## Campaign Design

| Use case | Recommended posture |
|---|---|
| New launch | Broad or simple targeting, multiple creative concepts, Maximum Delivery or suitable automated bidding |
| Conversion scaling | Stable event, adequate budget, Cost Cap only after signal exists |
| Retargeting | Shorter windows for high intent; separate from prospecting if reporting matters |
| Creative testing | One clear hypothesis per ad group or test cell |

## Measurement

Watch both platform and business metrics:

- CPM, CTR, CPC, video views, hold rate, CPA/ROAS.
- Landing-page behavior, checkout/form quality, app events.
- Comment quality and qualitative fit.

## Common Mistakes

- Reusing polished brand videos without adapting to TikTok.
- Testing too many small variants with too little budget.
- Optimizing for clicks when the business needs conversions.
- Ignoring comments and qualitative feedback.
