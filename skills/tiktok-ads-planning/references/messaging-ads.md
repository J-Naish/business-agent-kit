# Messaging Ads

Messaging Ads are designed to start conversations in supported messaging destinations. Availability and destinations can vary by market and account.

Current official framing distinguishes:

| Type | Destination |
|---|---|
| Direct Messaging Ads | Keep users in TikTok and open TikTok Direct Messages |
| Instant Messaging Ads | Send users to supported third-party apps such as WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, or Zalo where available |

## Best Uses

- Service businesses that sell through chat.
- High-consideration products needing consultation.
- Local businesses with fast response capacity.
- Lead qualification before booking or purchase.
- Markets where messaging commerce is common.

## Planning Inputs

| Input | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Messaging destination | Determines eligibility and user experience |
| Response team | Slow replies waste demand |
| Qualification flow | Converts conversations into useful leads |
| CRM or inbox routing | Prevents lost conversations |
| Policy category | Some industries need stricter review |

## Creative Guidance

- Make the reason to message explicit.
- Use a concrete prompt: quote, availability, recommendation, booking, product fit.
- Avoid vague CTAs like "message us" without a benefit.
- Set expectations for response timing and next step.

## Measurement

Track conversation starts, cost per conversation, qualified conversations, bookings, purchases, response time, and close rate.

## Common Mistakes

- Launching without a staffed inbox.
- Treating all conversations as equal.
- Not defining qualification criteria.
- Using messaging for simple purchases where a direct landing page would be better.
- Choosing a destination before confirming policy, market, and inbox staffing.
