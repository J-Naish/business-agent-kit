# Branded Mission

Branded Mission is TikTok's managed solution for crowdsourcing creator content based on a brand brief, then using selected creator videos in paid media. Availability varies by market and account.

Branded Mission can be paired with Branded Effects and may support lower-friction effect creation through Quick Effect where available. Confirm current managed setup options before promising effect participation.

## Best Uses

- Need many creator-style assets quickly.
- Brand wants participation from creators beyond a traditional influencer buy.
- Campaign needs authentic videos for paid amplification.
- Top-performing creator submissions can become paid ads.

## Planning Inputs

| Input | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Mission brief | Determines creator quality and compliance |
| Eligibility and market | Branded Mission is not universally available |
| Creator incentives | Affects participation quality |
| Usage rights | Needed for paid amplification |
| Approval flow | Brand and TikTok review can affect timing |

## Brief Guidance

- Explain the product, audience, and desired behavior.
- Give creators a clear prompt but leave room for native interpretation.
- State required claims, prohibited claims, disclosure language, and visual requirements.
- If the mission requires visible branding, specify logo/product visibility rules clearly; TikTok-managed briefs may enforce minimum visibility requirements.
- Provide examples, but do not over-script.
- Define selection criteria and paid usage rights.

## Measurement

Track submissions, approved videos, paid-media performance, creator diversity, engagement quality, sentiment, and downstream campaign outcomes.

## Common Mistakes

- Brief is too vague or too restrictive.
- Rights and disclosure are unclear.
- Selecting videos only by polish rather than performance potential.
- No plan to use winning videos in paid campaigns.
