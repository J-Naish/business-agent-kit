# Reach & Frequency

Reach & Frequency is TikTok's reservation buying option for planned reach, frequency, and forecasted delivery. Use it when the campaign needs predictable delivery rather than flexible auction optimization.

## Best Uses

- Brand awareness.
- Launches and tentpole moments.
- Campaigns with strict reach/frequency goals.
- Media plans requiring forecasted delivery.
- Premium in-feed options such as Top Feed where available.

## Planning Inputs

| Input | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Market and dates | Inventory and CPM vary by market and season |
| Target audience | Forecasting depends on audience size |
| Frequency goal | Controls repetition and waste |
| Creative volume | Multiple creatives reduce fatigue |
| Measurement | Brand lift, reach, video metrics, search/direct lift |

## Reservation Mechanics

| Mechanic | Planning use |
|---|---|
| Fixed Budget | Spend a defined budget and forecast reach/impressions/frequency |
| Fixed Impressions | Reserve toward a defined impression goal |
| Fixed Reach / Reach Percentage | Reserve toward a defined reach target |
| Advance reservation | TikTok guidance allows fixed CPM inventory reservation up to 225 days ahead where available |
| Tentative reservation | Secure CPM/inventory during planning, then finalize purchase before expiration |

## Operating Guidance

- Confirm availability and booking windows with TikTok or the buying platform.
- Use Campaign Forecaster or account planning tools where available before finalizing a reservation.
- Avoid judging R&F only by last-click conversions.
- Pair with auction retargeting or conversion campaigns when the campaign needs downstream action.
- Keep creative native; reservation inventory does not rescue weak creative.

## Common Mistakes

- Buying guaranteed reach without a creative refresh plan.
- Setting frequency without considering creative fatigue.
- Comparing R&F directly to conversion campaigns on CPA.
- Missing reservation deadlines or approval timelines.
