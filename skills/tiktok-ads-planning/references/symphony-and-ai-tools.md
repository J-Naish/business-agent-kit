# Symphony and AI Tools

TikTok Symphony is TikTok for Business's AI creative suite. Use it to speed up ideation, localization, video production, avatar-based variants, and refresh workflows, but keep human strategy, rights review, brand safety, and claims review in place.

## Main Components

| Tool | Use |
|---|---|
| Symphony Assistant | Brainstorming, scripting, trend/context research, and best-practice guidance |
| Symphony Creative Studio | Generate or remix TikTok-first video assets from text, URLs, images, products, or existing footage |
| Symphony Digital Avatars | Use stock or custom avatars for spokesperson-style creative where rights and disclosure are handled |
| AI dubbing / translation | Localize video and audio for additional languages where the tool is available |
| Symphony add-ons / partner integrations | Use Symphony capabilities inside supported creative and agency workflows |

## Planning Guidance

- Use Symphony to increase creative throughput, not to replace concept strategy.
- Start from a human creative brief: audience, problem, offer, proof, hook, CTA, claims, and prohibited content.
- Review AI output for product accuracy, policy, legal claims, music rights, likeness rights, and disclosure requirements.
- Use AI variants to expand hooks, structures, languages, and formats, not just to create near-duplicate edits.
- Compare AI-assisted creative against creator-shot and brand-shot assets. Do not assume AI output will outperform native creator content.

## Good Uses

- Turning product details into first-draft scripts.
- Creating quick hook variations for testing.
- Localizing proven creatives.
- Refreshing fatigued concepts with new openings or formats.
- Building low-cost prototype assets before creator production.

## Watch-Outs

- Generic AI creative can look polished but feel non-native.
- Avatar usage needs careful rights, disclosure, and brand fit review.
- Auto-generated claims can be inaccurate or non-compliant.
- AI localization can distort claims, offers, or legal language.
