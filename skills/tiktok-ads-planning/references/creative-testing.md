# Creative Testing

Use this reference when planning creative tests, refresh cadence, or creative decision rules.

## Testing Principles

- Test concepts before cosmetic variants.
- Isolate one major learning at a time.
- Make test differences large enough to change user behavior.
- Decide the metric and decision rule before launch.
- Do not read single-day noise as proof.
- Keep a creative log: concept, hook, creator, proof, offer, audience, result, comment themes.

## Official Baselines and Practical Heuristics

TikTok recommendation guidance commonly points to **3-5 unique creatives per ad group** to avoid fatigue. Use that as the baseline for ordinary launch cells.

Practitioner heuristics for larger accounts often require more throughput, but treat exact weekly creative counts as spend-dependent:

| Spend / scale | Practical posture |
|---|---|
| Small launch | 3-5 distinct creatives per ad group |
| Growing account | Weekly hook and concept refreshes |
| Scaling account | Planned creative drops, creator bench, remix winners |
| High-spend account | Creative pipeline becomes the main operating system |

## Test Types

| Test | Good design | Bad design |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | Same body/offer, several distinct openings | Different everything |
| Concept | Different persuasion angles | Same concept with new caption |
| Creator | Same brief across creators | Different creators with different claims/offers |
| Offer | Same structure, different offer | Offer plus audience plus landing page changes |
| Destination | Same ad to website vs Instant Page/form | Different creative and objective |
| Search creative | Same keyword theme, different answer/proof | Generic feed creative on high-intent query |

## Launch Cadence

For new launches:

1. Pick the business model and objective.
2. Launch a simple structure with enough budget for the event.
3. Use multiple distinct concepts, not many near-duplicates.
4. Let learning collect enough results unless tracking, policy, spend, or destination breaks.
5. Kill or refresh creative based on leading creative signals and downstream quality.

For existing accounts:

1. Identify the failure mode before adding new creative.
2. If hooks are weak, refresh openings.
3. If hold is weak, rewrite body/proof.
4. If CVR is weak with healthy hook/hold, inspect offer, destination, event, and lead/order quality.
5. Preserve proven learning when possible; avoid rebuilding the account for a creative problem.

## Decision Signals

| Signal | Use |
|---|---|
| CTR | Interest in the promise; can be misleading without CVR |
| 2s/6s views | Hook strength and early retention |
| Hold rate / watch depth | Body quality and pacing |
| CVR / CPA / ROAS | Lagging confirmation |
| Comments | Objections, confusion, proof language |
| Frequency | Saturation risk |
| Landing-page or form quality | Whether the click was useful |

## Creative Refresh Rules

- Refresh hooks when early-view metrics decline before CVR collapses.
- Keep winning bodies and test new openings.
- Replace concepts when hook refreshes stop working.
- Use Spark Ads and creator variants when proof/trust is the bottleneck.
- Do not pause stable ad groups casually; add/replace creative deliberately.

## Creative Research Workflow

1. Use TikTok Creative Center / Top Ads by market, category, objective, CTR, 2s/6s views, CVR, likes, and recency.
2. Save examples into pattern buckets, not "ads to copy."
3. Annotate the first 3 seconds, proof type, product reveal timing, CTA, text density, and comment themes.
4. Turn patterns into briefs: demo-first, comment rebuttal, before/after, comparison, founder POV, creator story.
5. Produce versions that fit the brand, offer, usage rights, and policy limits.
