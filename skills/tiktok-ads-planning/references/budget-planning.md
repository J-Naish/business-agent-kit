# Budget Planning

Use this reference to decide whether a TikTok Ads plan has enough budget and signal to learn. Numbers are planning heuristics, not promises.

## Budget Viability

TikTok guidance recommends budget levels based on the current CPA/CPR and optimization depth. Useful official-order heuristics:

| Optimization | Planning guidance |
|---|---|
| High-funnel / non-conversion events | Often needs more action volume; use at least the platform minimum and validate quality |
| Shallow conversion events | Can learn with more volume, but may optimize to low-quality behavior |
| Deep conversion events | Daily budget often needs to be around 10x current CPA |
| Install campaigns | Often needs around 20x current CPA |
| Search Ads Campaign | Daily budget-to-target CPA ratio matters; use enough budget for keyword learning |

If there is no current CPA/CPR, use local benchmarks only to estimate whether the campaign can generate enough meaningful actions. Do not promise benchmark CPM/CPC/CPA.

## Underfunding Diagnosis

| Symptom | Likely issue | Planning response |
|---|---|---|
| No delivery | Bid/target too tight, audience too narrow, policy/review issue | Relax target, broaden safely, check review |
| Clicks but no conversions | Event too deep, offer/landing page weak, tracking broken | Check event, LP, creative promise |
| Learning never stabilizes | Too little budget/volume or too many edits | Consolidate and reduce changes |
| Cost Cap does not spend | Cap too aggressive or signal weak | Raise cap, switch to Maximum Delivery, improve signal |
| VBO unstable | Value signal too sparse or dirty | Use cost-based bidding until purchase value is reliable |

## Bid Strategy Selection

| Situation | Better posture |
|---|---|
| New account / sparse signal | Maximum Delivery and simple structure |
| Stable CPA history | Cost Cap with realistic target |
| Hard bid ceiling | Bid Cap only if volume loss is acceptable |
| Purchase value matters | Highest Value / Minimum ROAS / Target ROAS where supported |
| TikTok Shop | Target ROI for stable GMV Max, Max Delivery for exploratory volume |
| Lead gen with weak quality | Optimize/report to qualified lead quality, not only CPL |

## Learning and Scaling

TikTok learning volatility commonly declines after about 25 campaign results or 7 days. During learning:

- Avoid targeting, bid, budget, objective, optimization-event, and major creative-volume changes unless something is broken.
- Use daily budgets for ongoing campaigns unless the campaign is a fixed-duration event.
- After learning, avoid sudden large budget jumps without a reason. A conservative example is to scale in measured steps, often around 20% at a time, then watch delivery, CPA/ROAS, and creative fatigue before the next move.
- Batch changes and keep a change log.
- Scale creative supply before forcing media spend.

## Benchmarks

Use benchmarks as sanity checks only:

- CPM and CPC vary by market, objective, season, creative quality, and placement.
- Cheap CPM is not cheap if comments, CVR, or lead/order quality are poor.
- Low CPC can hide a weak landing page or low-intent audience.
- Platform ROAS and GMV are not contribution margin.

Before scaling, estimate:

1. Expected impressions/clicks/conversions from the budget.
2. Required creative refresh volume.
3. Source-of-truth metric: CRM revenue, Shopify/orders, Seller Center net revenue, app LTV, or finance margin.
4. Whether the campaign can get enough events without optimizing to a shallower but misleading action.
